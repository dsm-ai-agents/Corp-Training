# Raw Discovery Call Notes (Unedited, Multiple Prospects)

These are rough notes taken during exploratory calls with prospective clients considering setting up a Global Capability Center (GCC). Notes are messy, informal, and un-synthesized on purpose — that's your job.

---

**Call 1 — VP Engineering, mid-size US SaaS company**
- "Honestly we've been doing everything through a staffing vendor for 3 years and quality is inconsistent. We want our own team, our own culture, but we don't know where to start."
- Worried about how long it'd take to get a real engineering org running. Mentioned a competitor already has "some kind of India center."
- Asked twice about IP ownership and whether code/data stays fully theirs.

**Call 2 — CFO, European industrial manufacturer**
- Kept steering conversation to cost: "What's the real all-in cost, not just salary — I've been burned by hidden setup costs before."
- Mentioned board wants a decision "this fiscal year."
- Skeptical of consultants in general — asked for reference customers, not slide decks.

**Call 3 — CHRO, US healthcare company**
- Big concern was attrition — "if we spend 6 months training someone and they leave in a year, what did we actually build?"
- Cares a lot about employer brand — "we don't want to just be another anonymous outsourcing shop to candidates."
- Asked about talent pipeline depth beyond entry-level hires — wants senior/lead-level talent too.

**Call 4 — COO, mid-market fintech**
- Fast-mover, wants speed: "If this takes a year to stand up we've already lost the initiative."
- Mentioned internal politics — a sibling team already tried "some outsourcing thing" that failed, so there's internal skepticism to overcome.
- Asked what the handoff/exit looks like once the center is running — doesn't want to be dependent on us forever.

**Call 5 — General Counsel, US logistics company (joined mid-call)**
- Focused entirely on compliance/legal entity setup, data residency, labor law exposure.
- Said their biggest fear is "signing up for something we don't fully understand legally and getting stuck."

---

## Loose Observations Across Calls
- Almost everyone brings up a "failed prior attempt" at outsourcing/offshoring as a reason for hesitation.
- Cost questions almost always come from Finance; culture/retention questions almost always come from HR; speed questions come from operational leaders (COO/CEO); risk questions come from Legal.
- Several prospects mention a competitor "already doing this" as a trigger for their own urgency.
- Nobody asked about the vendor's own case studies unprompted — always had to be shown, not asked for spontaneously (except the CFO in Call 2, who explicitly asked).
- Long-term ownership/exit ("will we be stuck depending on you") comes up more than expected.
