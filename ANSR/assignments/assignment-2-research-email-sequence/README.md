# Assignment 2 — Research-Driven Email Sequence

## Client
ANSR — global GCC (Global Capability Center) enablement and consulting firm.

## Background
Attached are raw, unedited discovery-call notes from prospective clients considering setting up a Global Capability Center (GCC). Notes are messy and repetitive on purpose — synthesizing them is part of the task. Note: these are fictional sample notes created for this exercise, not real client data.

## Dataset
[`inputs/discovery_call_notes_raw.md`](inputs/discovery_call_notes_raw.md)

GitHub path: https://github.com/dsm-ai-agents/Corp-Training/tree/main/ANSR/assignments/assignment-2-research-email-sequence/inputs

## Your Task
Based on your synthesis of the discovery call notes (plus your own research into ANSR and the GCC market), draft a **multi-email outbound sequence** targeted at ANSR's ICP (CXOs at global enterprises evaluating whether to build a GCC in India).

Your sequence should:
1. Define the target persona(s) and the trigger/context for outreach, based on the recurring themes and personas in the call notes.
2. Map a logical sequence (minimum 4-5 emails) with clear intent per email — e.g., problem-awareness, proof/case study, objection handling, urgency/CTA.
3. Write full copy for each email — subject line + body — grounded in the specific pain points and objections you identify through research (not generic outbound copy).
4. Specify cadence/timing between emails and any branching logic (e.g., what changes if the prospect opens but doesn't reply vs. doesn't open at all).
5. Note how you'd personalize each email per persona (CFO vs. CTO vs. CHRO) if targeting more than one buyer role.

## Deliverables
1. Persona/ICP summary (brief)
2. Full email sequence with copy, subject lines, and cadence
3. Short rationale note: which research findings drove which messaging choices
