[14:04] Helena Ashcroft:
Three things. One — our COO cost stack. Callum can go into detail, but net-net
we're running 18 to 22 percent above the retail peer set on functions that
should be commoditizing. Two — we've had an eighteen-month tech
modernization program that is materially behind schedule, and the honest
reason is that we cannot hire the data engineering and cloud security talent
we need in the UK. And three — three of our largest competitors have
announced India GCC scale-ups in the last twelve months. If we don't move,
we will be structurally disadvantaged.

[14:06] Callum Radcliffe:
Just to add to point one — the cost issue is not uniformly across functions.
Our commercial functions, our stores, our supply chain — those benchmark
reasonably. The cost drift is concentrated in Technology, Data, Finance
Operations and Customer Support. Which not coincidentally are the four
functions we're asking you to model for the GCC.

[14:07] Siddharth Mathapati:
That's helpful because it tells us the GCC scope is already validated by the
cost analysis. Let me ask about ambition. When you say "GCC" — are you
thinking of this as a shared services back office to run operations more
cheaply, or are you thinking of it as a capability center that will actually
own some of your product, engineering and analytics roadmap?

[14:08] Helena Ashcroft:
The Board decision was very clear on this. It has to be a capability center.
If it's just a back office, it doesn't solve our tech modernization problem.
The Chairman actually used the phrase "second home for the company", which
some people found dramatic, but it captures the intent.

[14:09] David Okafor:
Can I jump in on that? I need to flag the internal communications sensitivity
here. When word of this gets out — and it will — the union in Cork will
frame this as offshoring. We had a very difficult 2023 with the payroll
consolidation, and we cannot repeat that. So whatever we build, we need to
be able to say credibly that we are not moving existing UK or Irish jobs,
we are building new capability that we cannot otherwise access.

[14:10] Siddharth Mathapati:
Noted, and this is one of the most common patterns we see in first-time UK
GCCs. Two things typically help. First, in the first 18 months, the vast
majority of what you'll build in India is genuinely additive — teams and
capabilities you were failing to hire in the UK. That's an honest framing,
not spin. Second, we recommend committing publicly to zero UK/IE
redundancies attributable to the GCC for a defined period, typically 24
months. This gets tested in practice, and CFOs sometimes get uncomfortable
with it, but it's the single biggest de-risker of the internal narrative.

[14:11] Helena Ashcroft:
I'm comfortable with 24 months. Callum?

[14:11] Callum Radcliffe:
Yes.

[14:12] David Okafor:
Thank you. That will help enormously.

[14:13] Siddharth Mathapati:
Let's talk headcount and shape. What's your current thinking?

[14:14] Callum Radcliffe:
Draft numbers, and Helena has been pushing back on some of these. We're
thinking 600 to 800 FTE by end of Year 3. Tech and data around 55 percent,
Finance Ops 20 percent, HR Ops 10 percent, Customer Support 15 percent. On
the experience mix, our instinct is a pyramid that's heavy at the bottom —
roughly 65 percent junior, 30 percent mid, 5 percent senior.

[14:15] Siddharth Mathapati:
On headcount and functional split, that's directionally sensible and matches
what we see in comparable retail GCCs at year three. I do want to challenge
you on the experience mix, though, because I think if you go in with a 65/30/5
pyramid, you will not build the capability center you just told me you want
to build. In our dataset, GCCs that start below about 15 percent senior in
the first two years consistently underperform on ownership handover, and
the UK teams end up doing more of the design work, not less. Which was
supposed to be the whole point.

[14:16] Helena Ashcroft:
That aligns with my instinct, actually. What mix would you recommend?

[14:17] Siddharth Mathapati:
We'll come back with numbers after the benchmark, but I'd expect us to land
somewhere close to 45 percent junior, 35 percent mid, 15 percent senior,
5 percent leadership. The senior layer is what buys you the credibility to
actually own product and platform roadmaps. It costs more per head, but
your total delivered cost per unit of capability is lower — because you're
not rebuilding the design work in the UK.

[14:18] Callum Radcliffe:
Fair. Let's see the numbers.

[14:19] Priya Menon:
Siddharth, on locations. Our starting hypothesis is Bengaluru because it has
the name recognition. Do you agree, or is there a case for elsewhere?

[14:20] Siddharth Mathapati:
Bengaluru is likely to be the answer for the primary hub — but I'd caution
against locking it in before we've looked at the data. Two things have
shifted in the last eighteen months. Hyderabad has become materially
cheaper on infrastructure and their state government has a genuinely faster
single-window clearance — you can be operationally live about six weeks
sooner than Bengaluru. And Pune has emerged as the sharpest talent market
for Finance Operations specifically, with lower attrition than either
Bengaluru or Hyderabad. So the interesting question isn't "which single
city" — it's whether a primary hub plus a smaller spoke gives you better
economics and lower risk than a single-site build.

[14:22] Helena Ashcroft:
Interesting. Please model both.

[14:22] Siddharth Mathapati:
Will do. To be clear — I'm not recommending a two-city build in Year 1.
Splitting the Tech tower across two cities in your first year is one of the
top three execution risks in GCCs of this size. What we'd typically
recommend is single-city in Year 1, add the second city in Year 2 for
specific towers — most likely Finance Ops.

[14:23] James Whitfield:
Sorry to interrupt — before I have to drop for another call, can we cover

[14:24] Siddharth Mathapati:

This gives you the cleanest PE profile, standard for a first-time entrant.
Whether you set up one entity or two — one for Tech and one for Services —
is a tax optimization question we can come back on, but for planning
purposes assume a single Pvt Ltd.

[14:25] James Whitfield:

[14:27] Helena Ashcroft:
